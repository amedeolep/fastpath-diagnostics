Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our pilot programme

How long is the pilot programme?

The standard pilot period is 30 days, which provides enough time to thoroughly evaluate performance with your actual samples across multiple production cycles. Extensions may be available based on your specific evaluation needs.

What does the pilot programme cost?

Pilot programme pricing is customised based on your testing volume and chosen model (On-Site System vs. Express Service). Many pilots are offered at significantly reduced rates to qualified customers. Contact us for a personalised quote.

Do I need special equipment or lab space?

For the FastPath Apex® On-Site System, you need a standard benchtop space (61 x 61 cm), a power outlet, and internet connectivity. No special lab infrastructure is required. For FastPath Express® Service, you need no equipment at all - we provide sample collection kits and handle all testing.

How quickly can we start the pilot?

Once approved, On-Site System pilots typically begin within 2-3 weeks (allowing for equipment shipping and installation scheduling). Express Service pilots can start within days of approval - as soon as your sample kits arrive.

What if the system doesn't work for our needs?

The pilot programme is designed to be a no-risk evaluation. If FastPath doesn't meet your needs, there's no obligation to continue. For On-Site System pilots, we'll arrange equipment pickup at no charge.

What training is provided?

All On-Site System pilots include a 1-day on-site training and certification for your QC team. Express Service users receive virtual training on sample collection and the results portal. Ongoing support is available throughout the pilot period.

Can we run FastPath results alongside our current testing?

Yes, we encourage this approach during the pilot. Running parallel testing allows you to directly compare FastPath results with your existing methods and build confidence in the system before making any changes to your release protocols.
