RTE Industry Challenges

Understanding the critical food safety requirements for ready-to-eat manufacturing

Listeria monocytogenes

The #1 pathogen of concern for RTE foods. Can grow at refrigeration temperatures and persist in processing environments, making environmental monitoring critical.

Critical Control Point

Post-Lethality Exposure

Products are exposed to the environment after kill steps, creating recontamination risks that require robust environmental controls and testing.

Extended Shelf Life

Many RTE products have long shelf lives, requiring thorough testing to ensure safety throughout the entire product lifecycle.

Regulatory Scrutiny

RTE facilities face heightened USDA/FDA oversight with strict requirements for environmental monitoring programs and corrective actions.

Regulatory Compliance Support

Our solutions help you meet the latest regulatory requirements

USDA FSIS

Meet 9 CFR 430 Listeria control requirements for RTE meat and poultry products. Our programs support Alternative 1, 2, and 3 compliance strategies.

  • Post-lethality treatment validation
  • Environmental monitoring programs
  • Finished product testing

Comply with Preventive Controls for Human Food requirements, including environmental monitoring for Listeria in facilities producing RTE foods.
