Seafood Industry Challenges

Addressing the unique food safety requirements of seafood processing

Vibrio Species

Naturally occurring in marine environments, Vibrio can multiply rapidly in warm temperatures, posing significant risks for raw and undercooked seafood products.

Histamine (Scombroid)

Histamine formation in certain fish species can cause scombroid poisoning. Once formed, histamine cannot be destroyed by cooking or freezing.

Listeria monocytogenes

A significant concern for smoked and RTE seafood products. Can persist in cold, wet processing environments common in seafood facilities.

Cold Chain Management

Seafood requires strict temperature control from harvest to consumer. Breaks in the cold chain can lead to rapid pathogen growth and quality degradation.
