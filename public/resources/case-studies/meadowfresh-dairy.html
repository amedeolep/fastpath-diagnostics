The Challenge

MeadowFresh Dairy, a family-owned processor serving grocery chains across the Pacific Northwest, faced a critical challenge common to dairy operations: lengthy pathogen testing times were forcing them to hold finished products in refrigerated storage for 48-72 hours.

This delay meant:

  • Products reaching customers 2-3 days less fresh
  • Increased cold storage costs of over $15,000/month
  • Occasional product waste when shelf life was impacted
  • Inability to respond quickly to retailer rush orders

"We were shipping milk and yogurt that was already 3 days old by the time it left our dock," recalls Sarah Chen, VP of Quality Assurance. "That's 3 days of freshness our customers were losing."

The Solution

After evaluating several rapid testing options, MeadowFresh selected the FastPath Apex System for its combination of speed, accuracy, and ease of use. The implementation included:

  • FastPath Apex System with automated sample processing
  • Complete test kit inventory for Salmonella and Listeria
  • 4-hour on-site training for QA team
  • LIMS integration with their existing quality system

The Results

Within the first month of operation, MeadowFresh achieved same-day release for all tested products. The impact was immediate and significant:

Quantified Benefits

Testing time reduction 48 hrs → 4 hrs (75%)
Cold storage savings $180,000/year
Product waste reduction 40%
Customer satisfaction Increased 22%
