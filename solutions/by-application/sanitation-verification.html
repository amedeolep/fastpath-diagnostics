Sanitation Verification Methods

Multiple testing approaches for comprehensive sanitation assessment

ATP Bioluminescence

Adenosine triphosphate (ATP) is present in all living cells and organic matter. ATP testing provides instant feedback on surface cleanliness by measuring residual organic material.

Time to Result 10 seconds
What It Detects Organic residue, biofilm, microbial contamination
Best For Pre-operational verification, real-time sanitation feedback

ATP Testing: Your First Line of Defense

Real-time feedback enables immediate corrective action before production begins

1

Swab Surface

After cleaning and before production, swab the surface using the pre-moistened ATP swab device.

2

Activate & Insert

Snap the swab to release the reagent and insert into the luminometer for reading.

3

Read Results

Results display in seconds as Relative Light Units (RLU). Compare to pass/fail thresholds.

4

Take Action

Pass results allow production to proceed. Fail results trigger re-cleaning and re-testing before production.

Setting Appropriate Limits

Effective sanitation verification requires well-defined pass/fail criteria

Surface Type Pass (RLU) Marginal (RLU) Fail (RLU)
Product Contact - Stainless Steel <150 150-300 >300
Product Contact - Plastic/Rubber <200 200-400 >400
Non-Product Contact <300 300-500 >500
Environmental (Drains, Floors) <500 500-1000 >1000

Note: These are general guidelines. We recommend establishing facility-specific limits based on historical data, surface types, and product risk profiles. Our team can help you develop customized ATP limits for your operation.

Best Practices for Allergen Verification

  • Test after every allergen changeover - Verify removal before running allergen-free products
  • Focus on hard-to-clean areas - Gaskets, seams, corners, and hollow shafts often harbor residue
  • Document everything - Maintain records for regulatory compliance and recall defense
  • Train your team - Ensure sanitation crews understand proper cleaning procedures for allergen removal
  • Validate cleaning procedures - Use testing data to confirm your SOPs are effective
